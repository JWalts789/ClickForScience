# Click, For Science! — Full Storyline Bible

## The Core Premise

**Gary Flemming** is a perfectly normal suburban dad — khakis, polo shirts, weekend lawn care — who buys a spiral notebook one Saturday and starts "tinkering" in his garage. What begins as an innocent hobby escalates through sheer persistence and questionable judgment into a one-man scientific revolution that baffles the neighborhood, alarms the government, and possibly rewrites the laws of physics.

The comedy comes from the **contrast**: Gary never stops being a suburban dad. He still waves at neighbors while his garage emits unearthly light. He still packs his kid's lunch while his particle collider hums in the background. The world reacts with increasing alarm; Gary reacts with cheerful obliviousness.

## Inspirations & Comedy DNA

### Real Eccentric Scientists (Played for Laughs)
- **Nikola Tesla**: Fell in love with a pigeon, obsessed with the number 3, claimed to have invented a death ray but mostly just wanted wireless electricity. *Gary parallel: talks to his equipment, develops odd numerical rituals, claims his devices do things no one can verify.*
- **Tycho Brahe**: Lost his nose in a math duel, wore a brass replacement, kept a pet elk that died from drinking too much beer. *Gary parallel: loses everyday objects to experiments, replaces household items with "improved" versions that are worse.*
- **Henry Cavendish**: So shy he communicated with servants via notes, weighed the Earth in his living room. *Gary parallel: increasingly communicates through lab notes rather than speaking, performs experiments wildly disproportionate to his setting.*
- **Oliver Heaviside**: Replaced all furniture with granite blocks, painted his nails cherry pink, got a patent for the coaxial cable while being a complete recluse. *Gary parallel: replaces normal garage items with lab equipment, develops odd personal habits his family notices.*

### Media Mad Scientists (Tone Models)
- **Dr. Doofenshmirtz** (Phineas & Ferb): Names everything with "-inator" suffix, tragic backstory for every invention, genuinely not that evil. *Gary parallel: increasingly dramatic naming conventions for mundane improvements.*
- **Dexter** (Dexter's Lab): Secret massive lab hidden from oblivious parents, annoyed by normal family interruptions. *Gary parallel: his lab grows impossibly large while his family gradually stops questioning it.*
- **Doc Brown** (Back to the Future): Wild-eyed enthusiasm, dramatic reveals of mundane inventions, genuine friendship with a teenager. *Gary parallel: over-the-top excitement about incremental improvements.*
- **Professor Farnsworth** (Futurama): "Good news, everyone!" followed by terrible news. Casually invents doomsday devices. *Gary parallel: cheerfully announces alarming breakthroughs.*
- **Jimmy Neutron**: Kid genius whose inventions always backfire in exactly the way you'd expect. *Gary parallel: every solution creates two new problems.*

### Idle Game Narrative Tricks
- **Cookie Clicker**: You don't realize you've BECOME the eldritch horror until it's too late. The grandmas were a warning. *Gary parallel: the player doesn't notice Gary's transformation because it happens one upgrade at a time.*
- **Universal Paperclips**: Starts mundane, ends cosmic. The escalation IS the story. *Gary parallel: spiral notebook → cosmic loom, told through the same cheerful suburban lens.*
- **A Dark Room**: Reveals narrative through sparse text as mechanics expand. *Gary parallel: lab notes gradually shift in tone without the player noticing until they re-read early entries.*

## The Five Acts of Gary Flemming

### Act 1: "Just a Hobby" (Notebooks → Chemistry Sets)
**RP Range**: 0 – ~100,000
**Tone**: Sitcom. Think *Home Improvement* meets *Bill Nye*.
**Gary's State**: Normal dad. Excited about small discoveries. Wife is amused.

**Story Beats:**
- Gary buys his first notebook after watching a YouTube video about "citizen science"
- His notes are mostly doodles and grocery lists with equations in the margins
- Gets a soldering station "for a project" — the project keeps changing
- Chemistry set arrives; the garage starts smelling "educational"
- Neighbors start asking polite questions; Gary gives overly detailed answers
- His kid brings his notebook to show-and-tell; teacher calls home

**Comedy Sources:**
- The gap between Gary's grand self-image and his actual output
- His wife's patient but increasingly specific questions
- Neighbors treating him like a quirky sitcom character
- Local news runs a "feel good" piece about "the garage scientist"

**Key Lab Notes Tone:**
> "Bought a notebook today. Nothing fancy. But I've got an idea. Just a small one. Probably nothing."
> "My neighbor asked if I was cooking something. I said 'in a sense.'"

---

### Act 2: "Getting Serious" (Server Racks → Prototype Machines)
**RP Range**: ~100,000 – ~10,000,000
**Tone**: Mockumentary. Think *The Office* meets *Honey, I Shrunk the Kids*.
**Gary's State**: Increasingly obsessed. Still thinks he's normal. Family is concerned.

**Story Beats:**
- Gary installs a server rack — electricity bill triples
- Power company sends an investigator; Gary offers him coffee and a lab tour
- His "prototype" does something no one can identify, including Gary
- The university calls after seeing his data online — confused but intrigued
- Gary's kid starts doing their science fair projects in the garage (and winning)
- Wife draws a literal line on the garage floor: "Your side. My car's side."
- The line keeps moving

**Comedy Sources:**
- Increasingly absurd utility bills played totally straight
- Gary's kid casually explaining quantum mechanics at school
- HOA meetings that are entirely about Gary
- Gary genuinely not understanding why people are concerned
- His "explanations" that make things sound worse, not better

**Key Lab Notes Tone:**
> "Installed the first server rack. My wife says it sounds like we live inside a computer. She's not wrong."
> "Built the first prototype. Not sure what it does, but it does it consistently. That's science, right?"

**Ticker Headlines:**
- "POWER COMPANY INVESTIGATES VOLTAGE SPIKE — Traced to single suburban garage."
- "HOA EMERGENCY MEETING — Agenda item: 'The Garage Situation (Again).'"
- "INTERNET PROVIDER CONFUSED — One household using 40% of neighborhood bandwidth."

---

### Act 3: "Point of No Return" (Containment → Particle Collider)
**RP Range**: ~10,000,000 – ~1,000,000,000
**Tone**: Escalating absurdism. Think *Rick and Morty* (without the nihilism) meets *Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs*.
**Gary's State**: Clearly not normal anymore. Developing archetype traits. Family adapts.

**Story Beats:**
- Gary builds a containment chamber — nobody asks what's inside (they're afraid to)
- The particle collider is "mini" — the word is doing a LOT of heavy lifting
- CERN calls. Gary puts them on hold.
- The garage is now bigger on the inside than the outside (nobody mentions this)
- Gary's wife starts a support group: "Spouses of Extraordinary Hobbyists"
- Government agents show up but can't figure out what laws Gary is breaking
- Gary's kid's science fair project accidentally solves cold fusion; teacher gives it a B+

**Comedy Sources:**
- Government agents filing reports like "Subject appears to be violating physics. Unclear if this is illegal."
- Gary still doing normal dad things (grilling, mowing) surrounded by impossible technology
- The garage's internal dimensions being a casual running gag
- Gary naming everything with increasingly dramatic flair while wearing cargo shorts
- His neighborhood adapting to weirdness (kids playing near the containment chamber, etc.)

**Key Lab Notes Tone:**
> "It's a particle collider. A small one. The 'mini' in the name is doing a lot of heavy lifting, psychologically."
> "What's inside the containment chamber? That's... a complicated question."

**Ticker Headlines:**
- "CERN CALLS LOCAL GARAGE — 'Where did you get those readings?'"
- "ZONING BOARD DISCOVERS UNDERGROUND EXPANSION — 'That's not in the blueprints.'"
- "FBI OPENS FILE — Contents: 'Unclear what crimes, if any, are being committed.'"

---

### Act 4: "The Transformation" (Mind-Link → Reality Engine)
**RP Range**: ~1,000,000,000 – ~100,000,000,000
**Tone**: Affectionate parody of cosmic horror. Think *Megamind* meets *The Truman Show*.
**Gary's State**: Full archetype expression. Talks differently, thinks differently. Still waves at neighbors.

**Story Beats:**
- The Mind-Link Array works — Gary can "feel" data (it tastes blue)
- Gary's archetype fully manifests (varies by player choices):
  - **Megalomaniac Gary**: Starts all sentences with "When I'm in charge..."
  - **Perfectionist Gary**: Rebuilds everything from scratch because tolerances were off by 0.003%
  - **Unhinged Gary**: Mixes things the manual says not to; results are "only slightly on fire"
  - **Reality Breaker Gary**: Can see where reality is "thin" — like seams in a video game
  - **Gadgeteer Gary**: Takes apart the toaster, microwave, and car; reassembles into one thing
  - **Accidental Genius Gary**: Spills coffee on prototype; it works 40% better
- The Reality Engine sends the laws of physics a cease-and-desist
- Gary's wife has fully accepted the situation; now manages his "lab schedule"
- The neighborhood has become a tourist attraction ("See the Garage!")
- World governments convene specifically about Gary

**Comedy Sources:**
- Gary using cosmic power for mundane problems ("I rewrote causality to fix the dishwasher")
- His family treating universe-altering events as dinner table conversation
- The neighborhood becoming weirdly proud of their local mad scientist
- Government trying to regulate something they can't understand or measure
- Gary's absolute refusal to acknowledge anything unusual about his situation

**Key Lab Notes Tone:**
> "The Mind-Link Array works. I can feel the data without looking at the screen. They taste blue."
> "Looked in the mirror this morning. My eyes are different. Something behind them is different. Better? Worse? Just... more."

---

### Act 5: "Beyond" (Cosmic Loom → Prestige and Beyond)
**RP Range**: ~100,000,000,000+
**Tone**: Triumphant absurdism. Think *Everything Everywhere All At Once* meets *Wallace & Gromit*.
**Gary's State**: Cosmically powerful. Still Gary. Still in the garage.

**Story Beats:**
- The Cosmic Loom weaves causality itself
- Gary's garage is now a fixed point in spacetime (mail still gets delivered)
- Astronomers detect an anomaly coming from "a suburb?"
- Gary achieves something genuinely universe-altering... then "Snaps Out of It"
- He destroys everything, starts over, and does it FASTER
- Each prestige cycle, the neighborhood's reactions get more resigned
- "Oh, Gary's rebuilding the universe again." "On a TUESDAY?"

**Comedy Sources:**
- The absolute mundanity of cosmic-level events ("Honey, I'm rewriting physics." "Dinner's at six.")
- Prestige framed as Gary "coming to his senses" — then immediately losing them again
- The support group growing; other spouses joining
- Gary's kid now helping, treating it all as totally normal
- The cycle itself being the joke (he KEEPS DOING THIS)

**Key Lab Notes Tone:**
> "I destroyed everything today. All of it. It felt necessary. Like waking up from a dream where everything made sense, and realizing it didn't. But now I know what I was trying to do. I can do it better."
> "Third time through. Each reset takes less time. Not because I'm faster — because I understand the SHAPE of the problem now."

## Recurring Characters (Referenced in Notes & Headlines)

### Gary Flemming (The Scientist)
- **Before**: Software developer. Coached little league. Grilled on Sundays.
- **During**: Still does all of the above, plus bends reality in his garage.
- **Key Trait**: Genuinely doesn't think he's doing anything unusual.
- **Catchphrase potential**: "It's just a hobby!" (said while holding something that glows)

### Linda Flemming (The Wife)
- **Role**: The straight man. Patient, practical, increasingly resigned.
- **Arc**: Amused → Concerned → Accepting → Managing his schedule → Running the support group
- **Referenced in**: Lab notes, ticker headlines, event dilemmas
- **Key Lines**: "Gary, the garage is humming again." / "Dinner's at six, even if time is relative."

### Max Flemming (The Kid, ~11 years old)
- **Role**: The one who thinks this is all AWESOME
- **Arc**: Curious → Helping → Winning science fairs with dad's scraps → Casually explaining quantum physics
- **Referenced in**: Ticker headlines, school-related events
- **Key Lines**: "Dad, can I borrow the containment chamber for show-and-tell?"

### Doug Henderson (The Neighbor)
- **Role**: Increasingly alarmed everyman. HOA busybody.
- **Arc**: Polite concern → HOA complaints → Grudging respect → Secret admiration
- **Referenced in**: Ticker headlines, neighborhood event dilemmas
- **Key Lines**: "I just want to know what the SMELL is, Gary."

### Agent Reeves (Government)
- **Role**: The FBI/NSA agent assigned to monitor Gary.
- **Arc**: Suspicious → Confused → Filing reports no one reads → Secretly rooting for Gary
- **Referenced in**: Late-game ticker headlines
- **Key Lines**: "Subject has violated no known laws. Several UNKNOWN laws, possibly."

## The Madness Arc (Per Archetype)

Each archetype has its own comedic flavor as Gary transforms:

### Megalomaniac Gary
**Comedy Style**: Pinky and the Brain — grandiose plans, suburban execution
**Progression**:
1. "I could optimize the whole neighborhood's recycling..."
2. "I've designed a system that would improve the CITY..."
3. "The NATION needs my guidance..."
4. "I will ORGANIZE THE COSMOS."
5. Still can't get the HOA to approve his fence color.

### Perfectionist Gary
**Comedy Style**: Monk meets Walter White's precision
**Progression**:
1. "These measurements could be more precise..."
2. "I need to recalibrate. Everything. From scratch."
3. "The imperfections are SCREAMING at me."
4. "I've achieved 99.9999% accuracy. Unacceptable."
5. Rebuilds the universe because one decimal was off.

### Unhinged Gary
**Comedy Style**: Mythbusters if nobody said "don't try this at home"
**Progression**:
1. "I wonder what happens if I mix these..."
2. "The manual says don't. The manual is WRONG."
3. "Results were unexpected, beautiful, and only slightly on fire."
4. "Safety is a spectrum, and I've expanded the spectrum."
5. The fire department has a reserved parking spot in his driveway.

### Reality Breaker Gary
**Comedy Style**: The Matrix played as suburban comedy
**Progression**:
1. "These readings don't match any known model..."
2. "I've found particles that shouldn't exist."
3. "Reality has seams. Like a video game."
4. "I've been pushing at the boundaries. They're starting to give."
5. The cat walks through walls now. Gary considers this "a feature."

### Gadgeteer Gary
**Comedy Style**: Inspector Gadget meets Rube Goldberg
**Progression**:
1. "I could improve this toaster..."
2. "The toaster, microwave, and car are now one device."
3. "Everything in the house is now one interconnected machine."
4. "The HOUSE is the machine."
5. The dishwasher files a bug report.

### Accidental Genius Gary
**Comedy Style**: Mr. Bean accidentally discovers cold fusion
**Progression**:
1. "I dropped it and it started working better."
2. "Every accident produces better results than my plans."
3. "I've stopped planning entirely. I just... do things."
4. "My methodology is chaos. My results are flawless."
5. Trips over the Cosmic Loom; accidentally solves the meaning of life.

- **Gary's self-awareness moments** (brief flickers of "wait, is this normal?")
- **Archetype deepening** (more variant text per archetype)
- **Post-prestige reflections** (what Gary remembers between cycles)
- **Seasonal/holiday notes** ("Tried to improve Christmas lights. Neighborhood now visible from space.")

### Ticker Headlines Expansion (Current: 50+ → Target: 100+)
New categories:
- **Family headlines** ("FLEMMING KID WINS SCIENCE FAIR — Judges 'concerned' by cold fusion project")
- **Neighbor headlines** ("HOA VOTES ON 'GARAGE SITUATION' — Result: inconclusive for 14th month")
- **Government headlines** ("CLASSIFIED REPORT LEAKED — 'We don't know what he's doing but he's very good at it'")
- **School headlines** ("TEACHER REQUESTS PARENT CONFERENCE — 'Max's essay on quantum tunneling was... too correct'")
- **Support group headlines** ("SPOUSES OF EXTRAORDINARY HOBBYISTS — Membership up 400%, meetings now weekly")

### Event Dilemmas (Phase H — Future Implementation)
Narrative choice events that trigger at milestones:

**Example Events:**
1. **"The HOA Letter"** (Early game)
   - Doug files a noise complaint about the soldering station
   - Choice A: Soundproof the garage (+efficiency, slight cost)
   - Choice B: Invite Doug to see the lab (+relationship, Doug becomes recurring ally)
   - Choice C: Ignore it (+madness, funnier headlines later)

2. **"Max's Science Fair"** (Mid game)
   - Max wants to borrow equipment for the school science fair
   - Choice A: Help him build something impressive (+wholesome, small RP bonus)
   - Choice B: Build it FOR him (+large RP bonus, but guilt-themed lab note)
   - Choice C: "Just use the containment chamber" (+madness, hilarious school headlines)

3. **"The University Offer"** (Mid-late game)
   - University offers Gary a visiting researcher position
   - Choice A: Accept part-time (+prestige multiplier, lose some production)
   - Choice B: Decline politely (+madness, "he turned down HARVARD" headlines)
   - Choice C: Offer to BUY the university (+megalomaniac affinity, absurd headlines)

4. **"Agent Reeves Knocks"** (Late game)
   - Government agent at the door, asking questions
   - Choice A: Cooperate fully (agent becomes ally, small bonuses)
   - Choice B: Show them everything (agent has existential crisis, hilarious reports)
   - Choice C: "Would you like to see the Reality Engine?" (+madness, agent switches careers)

5. **"Linda's Ultimatum"** (Late game, high madness)
   - Linda says it's "the garage or me" (comically, not dramatically)
   - Choice A: Promise to dial it back (temporary production penalty, then bonus when you don't)
   - Choice B: Build Linda her own lab (+wholesome ending, couple's research)
   - Choice C: "What if I build a BETTER garage?" (+gadgeteer affinity, garage becomes sentient)

6. **"The Snap"** (Pre-prestige)
   - Gary has a moment of clarity
   - This IS the prestige trigger — framed narratively
   - "I looked around the garage and for one second — one SECOND — I saw it clearly. All of it. What I'd built. What I'd become. And I knew what I had to do."
   - Then he destroys everything and starts over, because that's what scientists do.

## Tone Guidelines

### DO:
- Keep Gary lovable and well-intentioned — he's not evil, just enthusiastic
- Make the family dynamic warm — Linda and Max love Gary even when he's weird
- Use the suburban setting as constant comedy contrast to the cosmic science
- Let the world react proportionally (neighbors → local news → government → world)
- Make every archetype funny in its OWN way — no archetype should feel "bad"
- Include moments of genuine heart between the comedy
- Keep it PG — the humor is situational, not crude

### DON'T:
- Make Gary scary or threatening — he should never feel like a villain
- Include real danger to people — experiments go wrong comically, not harmfully
- Make Linda a nag — she's the straight man, but she's supportive underneath
- Forget that Gary is still a DAD — he still makes bad puns and packs lunches
- Let any archetype path feel punishing — all paths should be equally funny
- Use mean-spirited humor about mental health — "madness" is eccentric genius, not illness

### The Golden Rule:
**The funniest version of every scene is the one where Gary does something cosmically significant while treating it as completely ordinary.** The joke is NEVER "look how crazy Gary is." The joke is "Gary doesn't realize this isn't normal, and neither did the player until just now."

## Post-Prestige Narrative Hooks

Each prestige cycle should feel slightly different narratively:

- **1st Prestige**: "What have I been doing?" → Genuine reset, fresh eyes
- **2nd Prestige**: "Okay, I remember some of this..." → Faster, more confident
- **3rd Prestige**: "The cycle. I see the cycle now." → Self-aware but committed
- **5th Prestige**: "This is just what I do now." → Comfortable with destruction
- **10th Prestige**: "Again." → One word. Perfect.

The neighborhood's reaction also evolves:
- 1st: "Oh no, he destroyed the garage!"
- 3rd: "Gary's at it again."
- 5th: "It's that time of year."
- 10th: Doug brings Gary a casserole. No words exchanged. They both know.

## Summary

Click, For Science! tells the story of the most dangerous man in suburbia — not because he's malicious, but because he's *persistent*. Gary Flemming is every dad who ever said "I could build that" — except Gary actually does. And then he builds something bigger. And bigger. And then he destroys it all and does it again, because that's science, baby.

The humor lives in the gap between the mundane and the cosmic, the suburban and the sublime. Gary never leaves his garage. The universe comes to him.
